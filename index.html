WORLD INSTITUTE FOR RESEARCH IN ADVANCED SCIENCES

WORLD INSTITUTE FOR RESEARCH AND ADVANCED SCIENCES (WIRAS) , established in 2018, is a postgraduate teaching and research centre seeking to integrate humanities and social science with classical Islamic Studies. The institute aims at contributing towards a harmonious and sustainable human future. The world-class interdisciplinary educational approach of WIRAS collaborates theology, jurisprudence, rhetorics, history, linguistics, philosophy, literary, cultural and legal studies and other disciplines. The curriculum enables students and researchers to engage responsibly and find solutions for contemporary social challenges within the broad framework of Islamic law and ethics.

INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE ON ENGAGEMENT OF MUSLIMS IN PLURAL SOCIETY (ICEMPS-2021)

“There is no religion in the world has yet made respecting the faiths of others religions a necessary condition of its faith and witness,” wrote İsmail Raji al-Faruqi. But, as to the aftereffects of several intertwined and complicated events, the presence of “Islam” and “Muslim” in contemporary societies is being challenged. This conference will explore some of the ways how cultural and religious pluralism has always been a central element to Muslim societies and how it continues to be so today. We will look at how Islamic faith accommodates other religions and diverse secular ideologies through the historical, philosophical, philological, ethical and jurisprudential analysis of Islamic literature and Muslim societies. The conference seeks to identify the reasons for the social status quo of Islam and Muslims in the modern world, through a genealogical approach and will try to formulate policies and proposals to educate the modern societies about “(real) Islam” and to initiate potential collaborations with diverse communities for collectively developing a sustainable future.

ICEMPS will discuss Islam’s plural heritage and plans for reclaiming them in modern societies through a series of academic sessions on key themes. It also proposes an academic conference aimed at a larger audience, of policymakers, researchers, professionals working or interested in inter-communitarian development.

GUIDELINES

  • Paper can be written and presented in English/Arabic/Urdu Languages and submitted through EasyChair
  • Paper must be in Microsoft Word format, double spaced, '12' font size with Times New Roman.
  • The Arabic and Urdu fonts and styles can be arranged accordingly.
  • Full tentative title of the manuscript must be included in the paper.
  • The abstract should be not more than 300 words. Please limit the use of acronyms, abbreviations and references in the abstract.
  • Keywords for the article: 3-5 keywords are sufficient.
  • Referencing should be in standard format. We recommend the Harvard referencing system.
  • Those whose final paper is accepted are only allowed to present the paper at the conference.
  • The presenters are requested to brief the paper in PPT format while presenting in the session as separate attachments on the submission form.
